Projects

My most recent work is LiveStocks, an IOS app I created in XCode after not finding a decent way to watch my stock portfolio holdings. I considered this the perfect opportunity to get into IOS development and Swift, and spent several weeks intensively working on this project, which proved to be rewarding, as well as a major learning curve.
I'm looking into potentially update my app with a widget, to make use of the new functionality in IOS 14.

I started learning how to incorporate existing API to serve my own purposes. This is an implementation of PyImageSearch's Facial Landmark API, as well as their Face Alignment that uses the dlib and OpenCV libraries to detect faces in one image, and then output all their aligned versions as a collection of 68 points, outlining their main features.
I am hoping to eventually think of a way to analyse the similarity between 2 different faces, based on the type of their facial structure.

My favourite project by far was last year's PokemonBattle, which was a group effort with 2 other people. It was exam season, and even under extreme time constraints, I got to design, program, and review many major parts of the game.
Even though it was fairly tasking having to combine code with other people, it taught me the importance of clear, concise, and commented code. In the end, we achieved an amazing final product that impressed many.

This one was a small predecessor to our final project - a typing game with similar Pokemon Graphics. It's not much, but it served to familiarise our team with the Greenfoot IDE that we were required to use.
